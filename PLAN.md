# 8-Week Job Search Plan

Goal: land an offer at ~50 LPA (3-4 L/month) at a startup, by **2026-06-18** (8 weeks from 2026-04-23).

Time budget: assume 8–12 focused hours/week on top of current job. More if you can.

---

## Week 1 (Apr 23 – Apr 29): Foundation

**Outputs this week: resume v1, LinkedIn polished, 2 portfolio links, target list of 30 companies.**

- [ ] Rewrite resume with impact-first bullets (metrics, scale, outcomes — not tech stack laundry lists). One page. Tailor 3 variants: (a) full-stack senior, (b) AI/ML senior, (c) founding engineer / 0→1.
- [ ] LinkedIn: update headline to "Senior Software Engineer · Full-stack + AI/ML · 0→1 & scale", update About with a 3-line pitch, set "Open to work" (recruiter-only visibility).
- [ ] Pick 2 strongest shipped projects → write a short case study for each (problem, your role, architecture sketch, outcome/metric). Host on a personal site or GitHub README.
- [ ] Build a **target list of 30 companies** using OPPORTUNITIES.md. Tier them: T1 = dream (10), T2 = great (10), T3 = backup (10).
- [ ] Decide your **salary floor**: what's the minimum cash you'd accept? Write it down. Target: 42 LPA floor, 50 LPA ask, 58 LPA dream.

---

## Week 2 (Apr 30 – May 6): Warm-up + first applications

**Outputs: 10 applications sent, 5 referrals asked for.**

- [ ] Apply to all 10 T3 (backup) companies first — these are your interview-practice reps, not your target offers.
- [ ] For 5 T1/T2 companies, find an engineer or EM on LinkedIn and send a referral DM (template below).
- [ ] Start system-design warm-up: skim "Designing Data-Intensive Applications" ch. 1–3 + one mock design per day on excalidraw.
- [ ] Schedule 2 mock interviews on Pramp / interviewing.io for weeks 3–4.

**Referral DM template:**
> Hi [name], I came across your work on [specific project/post] — [1 sentence of genuine comment]. I'm a senior engineer with 5y across full-stack + AI/ML, currently exploring [company]'s [team/area]. Would you be open to a referral, or could you point me to the right person? Happy to share a resume and a short case study. No pressure either way.

---

## Week 3 (May 7 – May 13): Volume + first interviews

**Outputs: 20 total applications, 3–5 first-round interviews scheduled.**

- [ ] Apply to all 10 T2 companies.
- [ ] DSA warm-up: 2 problems/day on LeetCode, focus on patterns (two-pointer, sliding window, BFS/DFS, heap, DP-intro). Target company tags from target list.
- [ ] For any first-round invite, research the company **deeply**: last funding round, who leads eng, recent blog posts, any controversies.
- [ ] Update [APPLICATIONS.md](./APPLICATIONS.md) after every application — date, company, channel (direct/referral/agency), role, status.

---

## Week 4 (May 14 – May 20): T1 push + interview loops

**Outputs: T1 applications out, mid-loop on 2+ companies.**

- [ ] Apply to all 10 T1 companies — only after your resume and pitch are battle-tested by weeks 2–3.
- [ ] System design: one full mock per week (45 min). Topics: URL shortener, rate limiter, feed ranking, chat, file upload, payment flow.
- [ ] Behavioral prep: write STAR-format stories for (a) hardest bug, (b) 0→1 launch, (c) scaling/perf win, (d) disagreement with a leader, (e) failure + recovery.

---

## Week 5 (May 21 – May 27): Mid-loop focus

**Outputs: onsites/final rounds at 2–3 companies.**

- [ ] Cut noise — stop applying to new T3/T2 unless your pipeline dries up. Focus on closing loops.
- [ ] Practice **compensation conversations** out loud. Never give a number first. If pressed: "I'm targeting 50 LPA cash base; total comp depends on equity and cash mix — happy to discuss after I understand the role better."
- [ ] Ask every recruiter for the band upfront. If they dodge twice, walk.

---

## Week 6 (May 28 – Jun 3): Offers + leverage

**Outputs: 1+ offer in hand, others accelerated.**

- [ ] When offer #1 lands, immediately email other in-flight loops: "I've received an offer I'm evaluating with a decision date of [date]. Is there any way to accelerate my process with you?" This is the single highest-leverage move in the whole search.
- [ ] Do NOT accept offer #1 same day. Ask for 1 week. Use that week.
- [ ] If offer is below 42 LPA floor: counter with "I was targeting 50 — is there flexibility?" Don't apologize, don't over-explain.

---

## Week 7 (Jun 4 – Jun 10): Negotiate

**Outputs: counter-offers, best-and-final from 1–2 companies.**

- [ ] Leverage competing offers honestly. Never lie about numbers — recruiters talk.
- [ ] Ask for: base, signing bonus, equity refresh, relocation (if applicable), start-date flexibility. Base cash matters most for your target.
- [ ] If stuck at 45 LPA from best option: evaluate whether to take it (still a 2x-ish jump) or hold and keep interviewing. Bird-in-hand usually wins unless pipeline is genuinely strong.

---

## Week 8 (Jun 11 – Jun 18): Close

**Output: signed offer at or near 50 LPA.**

- [ ] Accept in writing only after you've seen the written offer letter with all numbers.
- [ ] Notice period at current job: start planning transition.
- [ ] Log lessons learned in this repo for next time.

---

## Daily rhythm (weekdays)

- 30 min morning: 1 LeetCode + 10 min company research
- 30 min evening: applications + referral outreach
- 1 interview / mock per week minimum

## Weekend rhythm

- 2–3 hours: system design mock + behavioral story polish
- 1 hour: review the week, update APPLICATIONS.md, re-tier companies if new info

---

## Tripwires

If by **end of week 4** you have <3 first-round interviews: your resume or targeting is the bottleneck, not your skills. Get 3 people to review the resume that week.

If by **end of week 6** you have 0 offers in the 42–50 LPA band: expand to US remote contracts (Turing-tier) in parallel, OR reconsider whether the target is realistic for this market cycle and adjust floor to 38 LPA.

If you cross **week 8** without an offer: don't panic-accept. Reset, diagnose (was it the pipeline, the interviews, or the comp ask?), and plan weeks 9–12.

---

## Prep resources

- **System design:** "Designing Data-Intensive Applications" (Kleppmann), bytebytego.com, excalidraw.com for sketching.
- **DSA:** NeetCode 150, LeetCode company-tagged lists.
- **Behavioral:** "The STAR Method" articles; rehearse out loud, don't just read.
- **Comp data:** levels.fyi (India filter), ambitionbox.com, glassdoor.com, r/developersIndia salary threads.
- **Resume review:** r/EngineeringResumes, a peer or ex-manager, optionally a paid reviewer ($50–100).
